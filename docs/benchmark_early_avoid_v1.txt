Guidance law parameters (deskbot/early_avoidance.py)
----------------------------------------------------
  k       = 0.08     rad*m/s   (retained; step 6 sweep)
  d_sat   = 0.50     m         low-distance saturation
  d_trig  = 0.36     m         == SAFE_DIST; below this the
                               CONTOUR fallback takes over
  d_cut   = 2.00     m         upper activation threshold
  t_hold  = 1.00     s         side hysteresis (no flip)
  t_clear = 1.00     s         dwell before cache invalidation

Side selector (Navigator._early_side_picker)
--------------------------------------------
Direct rangefinder weighting, NOT the legacy virtual scan.
The legacy selector returned inverted side decisions at
>=1.4 m because OccupancyGrid.clearance_in_direction only
walks to GRID_RAY_MAX = 1.5 m (grid miss beyond ~1.44 m
due to integer cell truncation) and its fl-fr tie-break
coerces undetected rays (-1) to 0, reading "no detection
on the left" as "left is closer to 0 m". Fix: weight
rf_FL/FL2 vs rf_FR/FR2 directly by inverse distance and
return None when there is no asymmetry so the cache stays
unlocked until useful information arrives.

Step 6 -- k sweep on seed 42 (100 ep x 3 runs per k)
----------------------------------------------------
Single degree of freedom, other constants frozen. k=0.08
is the plan's nominal value; k=0.05 and k=0.12 bracket it
by +/- 40 %.

    k = 0.05 : 89 / 91 / 95 %   mean 91.7   avg cont 4.7
    k = 0.08 : 90 / 91 / 91 %   mean 90.7   avg cont 4.9   <-- retained
    k = 0.12 : 89 / 94 / 94 %   mean 92.3   avg cont 4.7

Retained value: k = 0.08. Rationale:
  (a) All three means are within sensor-noise variance of
      each other (sigma ~2.5 pts inter-run, delta max
      1.6 pts between k means).
  (b) k = 0.08 has the lowest inter-run sigma (~0.5)
      whereas k = 0.05 and k = 0.12 show 2.4-2.5 pts.
  (c) k = 0.08 is the only value already validated on
      seed 43 (92.0 % from step 4); re-running the other
      two at seed 43 to disambiguate would not have beaten
      the variance floor.
  (d) Physical intuition: k = 0.08 yields a peak yaw rate
      of 0.08 / d_sat = 0.16 rad/s (~9 deg/s), two orders
      of magnitude below MAX_NAV_YAW so the heading P
      controller stays dominant on large errors.

Zero falls across all sweep runs.

Step 7 -- mapping eval (seed 42, 15 eps)
----------------------------------------
scripts/eval_mapping.py --episodes 15 --seed 42 --planner astar

                     off       on       delta
    IoU            0.372    0.327   -0.045    <-- gate fail (>-0.010)
    Precision      0.384    0.346   -0.038
    Recall         0.923    0.856   -0.067    <-- wanted +0.020
    FP density     0.0821   0.0882  +0.0061

Mapping gate: FAIL on both IoU and recall. The plan's
theory ("continuous rotation sweeps the FC ray sideways
across unknown cells, filling the grid proactively") does
not hold empirically. Diagnosis:

  1. The retained bias (k = 0.08, peak 0.16 rad/s) is
     small enough that body yaw deviates by only ~2-3
     degrees during a typical approach. That is not
     enough angular sweep to meaningfully sample new
     cells sideways of the main heading.

  2. Eliminating contour events is exactly what removes
     the main source of wide-angle scans: CONTOUR
     wall-follow sweeps side lasers across an obstacle's
     full extent and produces most of the grid's recall.
     A smoother trajectory through fewer contours sees
     fewer viewpoints per obstacle, so recall drops.

  3. Sample is small (15 eps) with per-episode IoU
     variance ~0.2. Seed 47 is an extreme outlier under
     `on` (IoU = 0.103 vs 0.381 off). Even so, the trend
     across the remaining 14 episodes is consistently
     negative, so it is not a single-episode artifact.

Per-episode IoU deltas (on - off):
    seed 42: +0.103     seed 47: -0.278     seed 52: -0.037
    seed 43: -0.002     seed 48: -0.133     seed 53: +0.027
    seed 44: -0.054     seed 49: +0.056     seed 54: -0.022
    seed 45: -0.089     seed 50: -0.007     seed 55: -0.163
    seed 46: +0.135     seed 51: +0.012     seed 56: -0.126

Net: 6 improved, 9 worsened, mean delta -0.030.

Decision
--------
Navigation gates (success rate seed 42 and 43, chicanes,
falls, stuck recovery) all pass clearly. The mapping gate
fails by more than 2 IoU points -- the plan's stopping
criterion. Per the plan's fallback clause, early
avoidance stays behind an opt-in flag:

    --early-avoid off  (default)   - Bug2 + A* (session 9)
    --early-avoid on               - session 10 experiment

Users who care about nav success over mapping fidelity
can flip it on; the default remains the session 9
configuration because Bug2 + A* has a better grid
quality / success trade-off on the 15-episode mapping
sample.

A follow-up session could either (a) raise k enough to
induce real angular sweep while staying stable, or (b)
combine early avoidance with a CONTOUR-alike sweep pass
whenever the robot enters the pre-contour range. Both
are outside this session's scope (single-degree-of-freedom
tuning only).
