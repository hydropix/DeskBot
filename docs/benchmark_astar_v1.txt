Source of run-to-run variance
-----------------------------

scripts/benchmark_random.py does not reseed the SensorModel RNG per
episode. Sensor noise (accel, gyro, encoder quantization) is therefore
drawn from whatever numpy global state the worker process has at the
moment the episode begins. This introduces ~±3-5 pts of variance at
100 episodes. Both planners are subject to the same noise source, so
the delta is meaningful even though the absolute numbers jitter. A
proper fix — threading an ep_seed-derived RNG into SensorModel — is
out of scope for this planner change but noted as follow-up.

With the two wall/non-wall splits unmeasured but the global success
comfortably above the gate and no regression in falls, A* v1 is
adopted as an opt-in planner behind `--planner astar`. Bug2 remains
the default until the mapping sanity check in step 7 is clean.

Notes & non-regressions
-----------------------

- The initial implementation used path_initial_tangent with
  look_ahead = 4 cells (≈32 cm). On 50-episode runs A* regressed by
  4 pts vs Bug2 with double the stuck events. Diagnosis: a 4-cell
  look-ahead on an 8-connected staircase path is too noisy; a single
  diagonal step near the start can flip the measured tangent and
  commit the wall-follower to the wrong side. Fixed by widening the
  look-ahead to 10 cells (≈80 cm), well below the full path length
  but long enough to capture the bulk detour direction. No other
  constant was touched.

- No change to Bug2's FSM (states, transitions, watchdogs), no new
  rustine over GroundGeometry, no new safety layer. The only delta
  is the addition of `_contour_side_from_astar` plus the `use_astar`
  flag on Navigator. Virtual scan is still reachable verbatim.

- Zero new runtime dependencies (heapq + numpy only).

Step 7 — mapping impact (eval_mapping.py, 15 eps, seed 42)
----------------------------------------------------------

                     Bug2        A*         delta
    IoU            0.384       0.380      -0.004
    Precision      0.395       0.399      +0.004
    Recall         0.928       0.889      -0.039
    FP density     0.0841      0.0796     -0.0045

Interpretation: aggregate mapping quality is equivalent (IoU delta
−0.4 pt is well within the ±1-pt gate and inside run-to-run
variance). A* drops recall by ~4 pts because the route differences
slightly change which surfaces the robot sees during the 15-second
episodes; precision and FP density actually improve marginally. No
single-seed regression above 1 IoU point was observed in the
aggregated sample.
